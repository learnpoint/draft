Discuss the impact of human activities on the carbon cycle. What are the potential consequences for the environment?

Human activities, such as burning fossil fuels, deforestation, and industrial processes, have significantly altered the carbon cycle. These activities increase the concentration of carbon dioxide (CO₂) in the atmosphere, leading to enhanced greenhouse effect and global warming. Deforestation reduces the number of trees available to absorb CO₂, further exacerbating the problem. The potential consequences of these changes include: Climate Change: Increased global temperatures, altered weather patterns, and more frequent extreme weather events. Ocean Acidification: Higher CO₂ levels lead to more CO₂ being absorbed by oceans, causing acidification that affects marine life. Loss of Biodiversity: Changing climates and habitats can lead to the extinction of species unable to adapt quickly enough.

Human activities, such as burning fossil fuels, deforestation, and industrial processes, have significantly altered the carbon cycle. These activities increase the concentration of carbon dioxide (CO₂) in the atmosphere, leading to enhanced greenhouse effect and global warming. The potential consequences of these changes include: Climate Change: Increased global temperatures, altered weather patterns, and more frequent extreme weather events.
