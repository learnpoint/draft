Photosynthesis quiz

Photosynthesis is the process that allows plants to make their own food using sunlight. It happens in the leaves, where special cells contain chlorophyll, a green pigment that captures sunlight. The plant takes in carbon dioxide from the air through tiny openings in its leaves and absorbs water from the soil through its roots. Using the energy from sunlight, the plant transforms these ingredients into food in the form of sugars. As a result, oxygen is released into the air, which is essential for humans and animals to breathe.
This process is important because it provides food for plants, which in turn feed other living things. It also helps clean the air by reducing carbon dioxide and releasing oxygen. Without photosynthesis, life on Earth would not exist as we know it.
